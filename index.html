Estate Manta Cliff

on the cliffs of Diamond Beach

A sanctuary where bamboo roofs are shaped like manta rays, canvas tents are anchored in rammed earth, and a 53-metre cliff falls away to a private beach below.

The estate

Designed by an award-winning Balinese architectural practice known for poetic, place-rooted work. Canvas tents stretched over timber and rammed-earth foundations. Every suite opens to a private terrace facing the sea. The 53-metre cliff is the organising feature: a meditative stair descent leads to a private beach that feels like a secret.

The accommodation

Three tent-suite types across the terrain. Premium Type A (62 m², king bed) for singles or couples. Premium Type B (98 m², two queens or one king) for friends or families. VIP Cliffside Suite (130 m², two kings, two bathrooms, living room). Organic linens, locally sourced limestone walls, artisanal interiors.

Signature spaces for retreat programming

  • Manta-shaped yoga shala on the cliff — open-air, capacity ~25 for asana
  • Tea temple for breathwork, sound healing, smaller circles (up to 12)
  • Ocean-view infinity pool and cliffside spa for integration and rest
  • Private beach via the cliff stairs — for closing ceremonies, fire circles, swims
  • Farm-to-table restaurant with retreat-tuned menus

Typical experiences

  • Daily yoga, breathwork, sound in the shala and tea temple
  • Snorkelling or diving at Manta Point (world-class manta ray cleaning station)
  • Cliffside spa treatments
  • Day trips to Kelingking Beach, Atuh, Diamond Beach
  • Sunset rituals on the cliff, sunrise meditation at the tea temple
Best suited for

Design-forward wellness retreats — yoga, pilates, menopause work, breathwork, sound. Groups of 20–26 who want architectural immersion, not just accommodation.

Retreat leader FAQs

The most common questions we get from retreat leaders planning a week at Estate Manta Cliff — answered directly.

Is the full estate available for a private retreat buyout?

Yes. All 13 tent suites booked as one retreat, sleeping up to 26 guests. Estate Manta Cliff is designed around buyout — that's the primary way the property operates.

How much space is there for group yoga and movement practice?

The manta-shaped yoga shala is open-air and comfortably holds around 25 practitioners for mat-based practice — a little tight for a full 26-guest buyout, comfortable for 20. The tea temple fits up to 12 for breathwork, sound healing, and smaller circles.

Both spaces are covered, so inclement weather doesn't interrupt programming.

What's the minimum stay for a retreat?

Seven to ten nights is typical. The estate was designed around multi-day programming — shorter than seven compromises the arc retreat participants come for.

How do my guests reach the estate from Bali?

A 40-minute fast-boat from Sanur (Bali) to Nusa Penida, followed by a road transfer of roughly 45 minutes across the island. Group transfers are arranged as part of the buyout.

Total transit from Bali airport: approximately 3 hours including the drive to Sanur.

Can you accommodate specific dietary needs — vegan, gluten-free, Ayurvedic, allergies?

Yes. The farm-to-table kitchen works with retreat leaders in advance to design menus — plant-based, gluten-free, Ayurvedic, sugar-free, nut-free, anything the group requires. Fresh ingredients are sourced locally where possible.

Is the WiFi reliable enough for online components or hybrid retreats?

Yes. The estate runs on high-speed Starlink, with coverage across accommodation and communal spaces. Retreat leaders have run hybrid sessions and recorded content on-site without issue.

Can I bring my own facilitators and teachers, or is programming provided?

You bring your own team. The estate provides the spaces, the environment, the staff (hospitality, kitchen, grounds) and the experience infrastructure — but your retreat programming, teachers, and facilitators are yours. Facilitator accommodation is included within the 26-guest capacity.

What's included in a full buyout versus extras?

Included: full accommodation, all meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks), hospitality and kitchen staff, daily housekeeping, use of all spaces (shala, tea temple, pool, spa pavilion, beach), airport transfers for the group.

Typically extras: spa treatments, specific off-site excursions (diving, specific temple visits), alcohol, private boat charters, additional dedicated practitioners beyond your own team.

How far in advance do I need to book?

Six months ahead for peak season (May–September, December–January). Two to four months for shoulder. The estate holds a small number of retreats per year by design, so dates get quieter by 9+ months out.

What about medical access and safety for remote Nusa Penida?

A clinic is available on Nusa Penida for routine needs. Serious emergencies require transfer to Bali — the 40-minute boat option, or helicopter medevac if critical. The estate carries comprehensive safety protocols and insurance is strongly recommended for all guests.

When is the best time to run a retreat here — and when should I book?

Best retreat months: April through early November — Indonesia's dry season. May, June, September and October offer the most reliable weather with fewer visitors on Nusa Penida. July and August are peak — beautiful, but the island is busier with day-trippers.

A bonus window: manta ray dive season runs July–October, which can enrich retreats that include ocean excursions.

Rainy season: November–March still operates but weather becomes variable. Some facilitators specifically choose this window for its contemplative quality.

Booking lead time: 6 months ahead for peak dates (May–September). 3–4 months for shoulder season. The estate runs a small number of retreats per year, so dates get quiet 9+ months out.

The estate

Set on 100 hectares within the ancestral lands of the Marapu — Sumba's indigenous culture, one of the last Neolithic megalithic peoples on earth. Eleven villas stretched along two kilometres of white sand, each hand-crafted with local artisan weavings and carvings. Solar energy, water conservation, local sourcing woven into the operation.

The accommodation

Three villa types: Studio, One-Bedroom, Two-Bedroom. Eight villas sit directly on the beachfront; three are set back in the gardens. Each with a plunge pool or direct beach access. Organic bath amenities, locally woven textiles, a sense of being held by the land.

Signature spaces for retreat programming

  • Beachfront yoga and meditation deck with the Indian Ocean as backdrop
  • Two kilometres of private beach — for breathwork at sunrise, closing circles at sunset
  • Surf-friendly reef for group sessions with the resident instructor
  • Indoor salon as weather backup and for workshop-style programming
  • Restaurant serving farm-to-table Indonesian menus tuned to retreat programming

Typical experiences

  • Private surf sessions — beginner to experienced
  • Horseback riding on the beach at sunrise or sunset
  • Visits to traditional Marapu villages and Sumbanese weavers
  • Snorkelling, paddleboarding, ocean swims
  • Beach yoga, meditation, breathwork at the waterline
Best suited for

Surf + wellness retreats. Yoga teacher trainings. Women's gatherings that want cultural immersion and remoteness. Groups of 15–22 who aren't afraid of a flight + drive.

Retreat leader FAQs

The most common questions we get from retreat leaders planning a week at Estate Barefoot Sumba — answered directly.

Can I book all 11 villas as a private retreat?

Yes. The full estate for up to 22 guests at buyout. Partial buyouts (6–8 villas) are sometimes possible in shoulder season — ask for availability.

What are the programming spaces for yoga and group sessions?

A beachfront yoga and meditation deck that holds around 20–22 comfortably — the entire group can practice together overlooking the Indian Ocean. An indoor salon serves as weather backup and for workshop-style programming (circles, writing, breakout work).

Two kilometres of private beach is effectively an outdoor studio — ideal for walking meditations, breathwork, sunrise/sunset ceremony.

What's the minimum retreat stay?

Five to seven nights. Seven is the sweet spot given the travel involved — Sumba rewards arrival with time to slow down.

How do guests get to Sumba from Bali?

A 50-minute flight from Bali (Denpasar) to Tambolaka, Sumba. From Tambolaka, a two-hour drive down to Lamboya on the southwest coast. Airport transfers are arranged as part of the buyout.

Total transit from Bali airport: about 4 hours. The remoteness is deliberate — Sumba isn't a casual-weekend destination, and that's precisely its value for retreats.

Is the WiFi reliable enough for hybrid or online components?

WiFi is available across the estate and is adequate for group email, messaging, and standard video calls. For large-scale hybrid broadcasting, speak to the team in advance — they can often upgrade bandwidth for specific retreats.

Can you accommodate specific dietary needs?

Yes. The kitchen handles vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free, Ayurvedic, and specific allergies. Menus are built with retreat leaders in advance. Local sourcing means seasonal variation, which the team communicates upfront.

Can I bring my own surf coach, yoga teacher, or other facilitators?

Yes — you bring your own team. On-site staff (including the resident surf instructor) work alongside your facilitators if you wish, or step back so your team leads fully. Facilitator accommodation counts within the 22-guest capacity.

What cultural experiences can I integrate into a retreat?

Visits to traditional Marapu villages — one of the last Neolithic-megalithic cultures on earth — with respectful guidance from community elders. Weaving workshops with Sumbanese artisans. Traditional dance performances at sunset.

These are offered as coordinated experiences rather than tourist stops — the estate has long-standing relationships with local communities and facilitates immersion thoughtfully.

What's included in the buyout versus extras?

Included: all villas, all meals, hospitality and kitchen staff, daily housekeeping, airport transfers, use of the beach deck and indoor salon, beach and pool access.

Typically extras: surf lessons, horseback riding, spa treatments, cultural excursions (village visits, weaving workshops), alcohol, specific off-site day trips.

What about medical facilities in case of emergency?

Basic clinics are available in Waikabubak (about 90 minutes away). Serious emergencies require evacuation to Bali — flights run daily and medevac is available if critical. Trip insurance with medical evacuation coverage is strongly recommended for all guests.

When is the best time to run a retreat here — and when should I book?

Best retreat months: April through October — Sumba's dry season. The shoulder months (May, June, September, October) often give the best balance: warm, stable weather, fewer visitors, and gentler pricing. July–August is peak with the strongest weather and the most consistent surf.

Surf-focused retreats: April–September for reliable swell. Some surf retreats deliberately choose November–March for bigger winter swells, accepting some rain as part of the trade.

Rainy season: Sumba is drier than Bali year-round. Even November–March sees less rain than you might expect, but expect heavier afternoon showers.

Booking lead time: 3–4 months ahead for peak (June–August). 2 months works for shoulder. For full buyout during high-season, 6 months is safer.
